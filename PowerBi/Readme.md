#  Retail Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

##  Overview

This project is a multi-page Retail Analytics Dashboard built using Microsoft Power BI.
The main aim was to explore customer behavior, revenue trends, and business performance, and turn raw data into useful insights.

Instead of just showing numbers, the dashboard is designed to tell a story — so anyone can understand what’s happening in the business within a few minutes.


##  Files Included

* `Retail_analysis.pbix` → Full Power BI dashboard
* `images of dashboard/` → Screenshots of all pages
* `README.md` → Project explanation

---

##  Dashboard Pages

### Page 1 — Business Overview

This page gives a quick summary of overall performance.

**What it shows:**

* Total Revenue, Customers, Avg Order Value, Loyalty Score
* Revenue comparison across categories
* Seasonal trends
* Top-performing items

**What I observed:**

* Clothing brings the highest total revenue.
* Winter season performs the best.
* Footwear has fewer customers but higher value per customer .

---

###  Page 2 — Customer Segmentation

This page focuses on understanding different types of customers.

**What it shows:**

* Revenue by age group and gender
* Spending patterns across demographics
* Payment method preferences
* Impact of discounts and subscriptions

**Key observations:**

* Customers aged 50+ (especially male) generate the most revenue
* Younger customers buy more often but spend less
* Older customers are more valuable and loyal
* Subscription increases loyalty, but total value is almost the same

---

###  Page 3 — Operations & Product Insights

This page looks at business operations and product-level performance.

**What it shows:**

* Revenue by purchase frequency
* Loyalty score by customer type
* Revenue across locations
* Customer satisfaction by shipping type
* Seasonal product trends

**Key observations:**

* Loyal customers contribute the most revenue
* Store pickup might have operational issues
* Montana leads in revenue, while Illinois has high loyalty

---

##  Important Insights

* **Footwear vs Clothing:**
  Even with fewer customers, footwear buyers spend more per person

* **High-value customers:**
  Male customers aged 50+ are the most profitable

* **Subscriptions:**
  Improve loyalty, but don’t significantly increase total value

* **Operations issue:**
  Store pickup may be affecting customer experience

* **Seasonality:**
  Winter drives higher revenue, while summer has lower cart values
